This report estimates the total "Global Addressable Market" (GAM) of Romanian descendants eligible for citizenship under Articles 10 and 11, and compares it to current institutional throughput.

## 3. Market Saturation Analysis (2012–2026)

| Track | Potential Pool (Mid-Point) | Total Registered (Actual) | Saturation % | Remaining Pool |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Article 10** | 3.7 Million | 42,309 | **1.1%** | **~3.65 Million** |
| **Article 11** | 7.0 Million | 733,922 | **10.5%** | **~6.26 Million** |

### Strategic Implications:
1.  **Article 10 Capacity:** At ~1.1% saturation, the restoration track for descendants of emigrants represents a large available applicant pool. The current census-based potential indicates significant future demand capacity.
2.  **Article 11 Queue Depth:** With ~10.5% saturation, the re-acquisition track is more established. The recent decline in registration coincides with Law 14/2025's requirements, suggesting that administrative gates, rather than pool exhaustion, are the primary filter.
3.  **The B1 Gate Impact:** The Romanian language requirement (B1) acts as a high-friction filter. If it remains a hard gate, the "realizable" pool—those both eligible *and* capable/willing to meet the language standard—is projected to be significantly smaller than the total potential pool documented above.

*Data Sources: 1930 Romanian Census, 1940 Soviet Annexation Records, Post-WWII Migration Statistics (IL/DE/TR).*
