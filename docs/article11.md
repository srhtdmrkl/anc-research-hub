Article 11: Restoration for Historical Territories

Article 11 is the most widely used provision overall, applying to individuals whose ancestors lost their Romanian citizenship due to geopolitical events entirely outside of their control.

Who it applies to: Former citizens who lost their status against their will, and their direct descendants up to the 3rd degree (children, grandchildren, and great-grandchildren).

The Historical Context:

This predominantly covers descendants of individuals born in the territories of "Greater Romania" between 1918 and 1940. When the Soviet Union annexed territories like Bessarabia (modern-day Moldova) and Northern Bukovina/Hertsa (modern-day Ukraine), the inhabitants were stripped of their Romanian citizenship overnight.
