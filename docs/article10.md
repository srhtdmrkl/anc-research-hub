Article 10: Restoration for Former Citizens

Article 10 applies to individuals who formally held Romanian citizenship at one point but lost it, as well as their immediate descendants.

Who it applies to: Former citizens and their direct descendants up to the 2nd degree (children and grandchildren).

The Historical Contexts:

The Turkish Diaspora: This is a major pathway for Turkish and Tatar minorities who emigrated to Turkey from regions like Dobruja or Ada Kaleh. They typically lost their Romanian citizenship when they surrendered their passports upon exiting or when they naturalized as Turkish citizens.

General Emigration: It also covers others who lost their citizenship due to fleeing the Communist regime, or other minority groups (such as Jewish, German, or Armenian populations) who were forced to renounce their citizenship when leaving the country.
